Lasagna

I'd love to write a lasagna joke here, but it's multi-layered and way too cheesy...

Ingredients:

  • Onion and garlic (chopped)
  • Olive oil, salt and pepper
  • White and red lasagna sauce
  • Mince (I reccomend beef, but pork works too)
  • Cheddar cheese
  • Lasagna sheets
    •

    Method:

    1. Begin by preheating the oven to about 180 degrees
    2. Returning to the hob, heat some oil in a suitable frying pan on a medium heat
    3. Once hot, add the onion and garlic (being careful not to burn the shit out of the garlic) and sprinkle on some salt and pepper
    4. After a couple of minutes, add the mince and fry until browned
    5. Once the mince has browned, add the red sauce to pan and simmer for 5-10 minutes on a lower heat
    6. Take the pan off the heat and prepare your oven dish. We are now ready to layer it up!
      • Note: The lasagna needs to be layered in a specific order in your oven dish. Ideally, you want 2 'completed' layers, but don't worry if it doesn't fit your dish - a single layer of each will be just as delicious!
    7. Layer your lasagna!
      1. Add a base layer of red sauce to the dish
      2. Follow the red sauce with a layer of lasagna sheets, being sure to cover any gaps as best you can
      3. Add a nice thick layer of white sauce onto the lasagna sheets
      4. Follow the white sauce with another layer of lasagna sheets (like a white sauce sandwich!)
      5. Add another layer of red sauce, then repeat steps 2, 3.
      6. Add a generous helping of cheese, and you're done!
    8. Chuck that thing in the oven (don't actually chuck it) for about 20-25 minutes or until the cheese has browned nicely
    9. Voila! You are done!
